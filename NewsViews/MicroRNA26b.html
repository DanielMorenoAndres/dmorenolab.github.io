MicroRNA-26b Attenuates Platelet Adhesion and Aggregation in Mice

  • Platelets malfunction lead to thrombosis. Manifold microRNAs (miRs) are critical to determine platelet function. One of them, the MiR-26b, is highly expressed in healthy platelets, but altered in disease.
  • A great collaborative job between researchers from Aachen and Maastricht universities, leaded Linsey Peters and Emiel van der Vorst found out that whole-body deficiency of miR-26b in mice accelerate platelet adhesion and enhances aggregation. Using intravital microscopy, we have help to check that platelets adhered faster and generate larger thrombi in MiR-26b-KO mice.
