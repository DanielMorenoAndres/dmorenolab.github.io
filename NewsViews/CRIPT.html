Biallelic variants in CRIPT cause a Rothmund-Thomson-like syndrome with increased cellular senescence

  • We collaborate in a study led by Lusia Averdunk and Felix Distelmaier (Department of General Pediatrics, Neonatology and Pediatric Cardiology, Medical Faculty, University Hospital Düsseldorf, Heinrich-Heine-University, Düsseldorf, Germany). The research reveals that all individuals with CRIPT variants meet the diagnostic criteria for RTS and also exhibit neurodevelopmental delays and seizures. Computational analysis indicates a strong facial resemblance between CRIPT and RTS individuals. Skin biopsies show elevated expression of senescence markers and activity in CRIPT-deficient fibroblasts. Both RECQL4- and CRIPT-deficient fibroblasts demonstrate normal mitotic progression, few mitotic errors, and only mild sensitivity to various genotoxic stresses. The study suggests that CRIPT is associated with an RTS-like syndrome characterized by neurodevelopmental delay and epilepsy, indicating shared molecular mechanisms underlying these clinical phenotypes.
