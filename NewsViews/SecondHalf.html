The second half of mitosis and its implications in Cancer Biology

  • Errors in chromatin segregation during the process of cell division are of extreme importance in the apparition and evolution of cancer. Traditionally, the origin of these errors has been assigned to failures in the mechanisms that drive the first part of mitosis, before the separation of the chromatids from the chromosomes during anaphase. However, recent research is reinforcing the importance in cancer biology of molecular factors and processes involved in the mitotic exit and the nuclear reformation at the end of mitosis.
